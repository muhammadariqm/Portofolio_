About Me

Hi! I’m Muhammad Ariq Mubarak, a Computer Engineering student at the Indonesian Digital Technology University. I have a strong interest in software development, particularly in web and mobile applications. Currently, I enjoy working on various projects involving HTML, CSS, PHP, JavaScript, and Dart.

I am skilled at creating dynamic and interactive web pages using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I also build robust server-side applications with PHP and manage databases like MySQL, MongoDB, and MariaDB. Additionally, I am diving into Dart for mobile development with Flutter, which is exciting due to its efficiency and flexibility in building cross-platform applications.

I am also interested in exploring Business Intelligence, learning about data analysis, data mining, and building efficient data warehouses. Whether developing small applications or large systems, I always focus on creating scalable, efficient, and user-friendly solutions. I also have expertise in using Figma to design appealing and functional user interfaces.

Throughout my studies, I have developed a strong interest in the field of cybersecurity, which is becoming increasingly important in today's digital world. I have explored various aspects of cybersecurity, from network security to threat analysis, risk management, and digital forensics. I am particularly passionate about ensuring data integrity, confidentiality, and availability through the implementation of secure systems and protocols.

During my learning journey, I have focused on identifying vulnerabilities in various systems, understanding attack vectors, and studying encryption techniques to secure communications. I have also gained experience with several tools and techniques used to combat cyber threats, including firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), penetration testing, and digital forensics. My exploration in digital forensics has allowed me to understand how to investigate and analyze digital evidence to trace cyber incidents and uncover malicious activities.

Cybersecurity is not just about protecting systems but also about cultivating a proactive mindset to anticipate and mitigate potential threats. I am excited to continue expanding my knowledge in this field and applying it in real-world scenarios to contribute to the security and safety of the digital environment.
